# Product Vision — Untangle

## Target user
People who struggle with executive dysfunction — including but not limited to
adults with ADHD — who have difficulty starting, structuring, or recovering
their day when plans fall apart. They know what they need to do; the barrier
is translating that into a workable, trustworthy plan they can actually follow.

## Core problem
When a day goes off-script, replanning requires enough mental overhead that
people give up entirely and lose the rest of the day. The cognitive cost of
"figuring out what to do next" is the blocker — not motivation or capability.

## Key principles
- **Voice-first, friction-last**: the fastest path from chaos to a plan is
  speaking it out loud. Every interaction should minimize reading and deciding.
- **Trustworthy output**: a plan that feels wrong won't be followed. Estimates
  and schedules must be realistic enough to act on immediately.
- **ADHD-informed UX**: calm visuals, minimal cognitive load, clear hierarchy.
  Show exactly what to do right now — not everything at once.
- **One screen, one job**: the planner shows the current moment and what's
  next. No backlog, no history, no context-switching.

## Explicit non-goals
- Not a general-purpose task manager or to-do list
- Not a team or collaboration tool
- Not a time tracker or productivity analytics dashboard
- Not a habit-building or streaks/gamification product
